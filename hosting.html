Hosting Coders Who Teach

What is Coders Who Teach?

Coders Who Teach is a volunteer-run meetup for people who teach programming to share experience and best practice. Most of our community teach in non-traditional education contexts such as meetups, workshops, bootcamps or coding courses (for adults, or children), many of them as volunteers. Our community is focussed on how we can grow and improve as teachers, so we can provide the best for our students and mentees.

Why might you like to host us?

If our purpose sounds exciting to you, you might like to host us! We hope that by growing our community, we‚Äôll not only be supporting a great group of coders who teach, but indirectly improving the educational experience of their students.

In addition, many tech companies host meetups as a way to upskill their engineers, engage the wider tech community and meet potential future hires. Our community of coders who teach are developers with a wide range of technical experience and the ability to share it with others - to mentor, nurture and develop future talent.

What happens at a Coders Who Teach meetup?

Sample agenda:

  • 6.30pm - doors open
  • 7pm - lightning talks
  • 7.30pm - breakout discussion sessions
  • 8.15pm - soft drinks and snacks
  • 9pm - politely kick everyone out of your office

Previous speakers include Abby Sassell on 'Creating a Coach', Tom Grand on 'Cognitive Load Theory, In Practice' and Anisah Osman Britton, founder of 23 Code Street, on 'Teacher vs Role Model' (see Eventbrite for a previous meetup at Twitter UK).
